Abstract Submission

Abstract & Symposium submission for the Multilateral Initiative on Malaria (MIM Society) 8th Pan-African Malaria Conference is now closed. The conference will accept late-breaking abstract submissions from 1-15 March 2024.

Submit Abstract

Abstract & Symposium Submission Guidelines

This is an open call for abstracts. Participant may submit an abstract for review via www.mimAfrica.com Authors may submit for oral, poster or symposia sessions.

The abstract submission deadline is January 31, 2024 (23h59 CAT).

Acceptance: Applicants will be notified of acceptances by February 21, 2024, and must confirm participation within a week of notification.

Accepted abstracts will be presented at the MIM Society 8th Pan African Malaria Conference (PAMC) 2024 on 21 â€“ 27 April 2024 in Kigali- Rwanda

Late-Breaking Abstract Submission: A limited number of late-breaking abstracts will be accepted between March 1-15, 2024. Please note that the late-breaking abstract deadline is not a second deadline for abstract submissions. We highly recommend that you complete and submit your abstracts during the early submission deadline to avoid the risk of disqualification.

Topics
Abstracts will be accepted for the following thematic areas:

  • Parasite and Systems Biology
  • Malaria in the Context of Co-infections
  • Social and Health Economics
  • Malaria Genomics (Human-Anopheles-Plasmodium)
  • Malaria Epidemiology, Modelling, Surveillance and Impact
  • Drug Efficacy
  • Drug Resistance
  • Immunology & Vaccines (Children, Pregnant Women and Adults)
  • Malaria in Pregnancy
  • Treatment and Community Case Management
  • Diagnosis and Reagents Development
  • Capacity Building, Leadership, and Governance
  • Control, Elimination and Eradication
  • Chemoprevention
  • Vector Biology and Control
  • Malaria in the Context of Climate Change
  • Phytomedicines, Pharmacology and Drug Development
  • Phytomedicines, Pharmacology and Drug Development
  • Implementation Science and Health systems
  • Bioethics and Legal Issues
  • Malaria and Diversity (Equity, Gender & Inclusion)

Instructions

  • Include the names, institutions and email address of all co-authors
  • Include all authors who contributed to the research
  • Ensure that the abstract includes:
    • Abstract title
    • Introduction and Objective
    • Methods
    • Results
    • Conclusions
    • Impact/Significance
  • Abstract body (including title, authors, etc) should be a minimum of 300 characters and maximum of 3000 characters.
  • Authors are encouraged to submit original work that has not been previously published. However, if the abstract presents further progression of previously published research, it will be considered for submission.
  • Abstracts should be submitted in English.

Key Details: Symposium Submission

Symposium Title and Theme:

Choose a clear and concise title for your symposium that reflects the central theme or topic. Ensure that the theme aligns with the overall conference theme, which for PAMC 2024 is "Grassroots Mobilization to End Malaria: Invest, Innovate & Integrate".

Symposium Organizers:

Describe the primary organizers or coordinators of the symposium. Include their names, affiliations, and contact information.

Purpose & Objectives:

Ouline the purpose, objectives, and scope of your symposium. Clearly state the rational or relevance of your symposium to malaria research, prevention, or control.

Highlight any innovative approaches, novel findings, or practical applications that will be discussed.

Symposium Format:

Specify the format of your symposium (e.g., panel discussion, oral presentations, case studies, etc.).

Indicate the expected number of attendees and duration of the symposium (e.g., 60 minutes, 90 minutes).

If applicable, propose any interactive elements (e.g., Q&A sessions)

Chair and co-chair, Coordinator/contact person & Speakers

Provide the names, affiliation, addresses and contact details of the Chair, Co-Chair, and coordinator/contact person.

Provide a list of potential speakers and their proposed topics. Include brief bios for each speaker.

Key Details: Posters Submission

Poster Design:

  • Create an eye-catching poster with clear headings, visuals, and concise text.
  • Include sections such as background, methods, results, and conclusions.
  • Use high-resolution images and graphs.
  • Ensure readability from a distance.

Presentation:

  • If selected for a poster presentation, be prepared to discuss your research with conference attendees.
  • Stand by your poster during designated poster sessions.
  • Engage with participants and answer questions.
